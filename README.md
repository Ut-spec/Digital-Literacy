# Digital-Literacy
This project was completed as part of the Digital Literacy course with the objective of
developing essential digital skills required for academic and professional growth. As a
Student Digital Ambassador, my role was to understand and demonstrate how to
effectively navigate the digital world, build a professional online presence, and promote
safe online practices. The project involved five tasks, including creating an infographic,
building a digital portfolio, exploring coding and collaboration platforms, practicing
professional communication, and studying cybercrime awareness. Each task
contributed to enhancing my understanding of digital tools and their real-world
applications. This project not only improved my technical awareness but also helped
me develop responsible digital habits that will be beneficial throughout my academic
journey

---

## Task Summaries

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic
- Tool: **Canva**
- Created a one-page infographic covering:
  - What digital literacy is
  - Safe internet practices
  - Professional online presence
- Focused on visual clarity and engaging design to convey key concepts.

### Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio
- Platforms used: **GitHub, LinkedIn, Kaggle**
- GitHub: Showcased coding projects via profile README
- LinkedIn: Built professional profile for networking
- Kaggle: Explored datasets and competitions
- Goal: Build a strong professional and technical online presence.

### Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms
- Coding: **HackerRank** – completed beginner-level challenges
- Collaboration: **Google Forms** – created a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz
- Developed problem-solving and collaborative data collection skills.

### Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette
- Drafted professional emails to:
  - A professor requesting assignment extension
  - Internship coordinator expressing interest
- Created a social media do’s and don’ts checklist to promote responsible digital communication.

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness
- Cybercrime type: **UPI/Online Payment Fraud**
- Created a 200–250 word case study describing how online fraud occurs, targets, and consequences
- Developed an 8-point “Stay Safe Online” checklist, including financial safety tips
- Learned practical steps to avoid cybercrime.

---

## Reflections
This project enhanced my understanding of digital literacy, improved my digital presence, coding skills, professional communication, and cybercrime awareness. It also prepared me to guide peers and promote responsible digital practices.
